ABOUT US

United for Impact

Connect and empower university students across the world to achieve their educational goals.

University students across the world face two major barriers to transformative learning. The first is limited peer exposure. Students often confined to interacting with their peers with their institution or major, stifling creativity, diversity of thought, and global perspective. The second is weak interdisciplinary training. Despite strong evidence that exposure to multiple disciplinary insights strengthens students’ problem-solving skills, most academic programs and institutions are siloed.

A 2020 University of Arizona study confirms this: students retain more and perform better when they actively discuss concepts with diverse peers.

But how can this kind of dynamic exchange across geography, culture, and disciplines happen especially among students who don’t know each other or share a prior network? We built KampuLynk to answer this question.

KampuLynk is an online educational platform designed to connect university students – across majors, institutions, and countries – to exchange ideas, collaborate on projects, and access educational resources to facilitate their growth. It’s a platform to connect with like-minded (or cross-disciplinary) students, a place to store and share academic notes, and a space to collaborate, chat, learn, and grow.

With Kampulynk, a computer science student at Columbia University can connect and exchange ideas with students with the same or different major(s) at say, the University of London, Peking University, or the University of Cape Town (South Africa), without being friends.
