ABOUT US

University students across the world face two major barriers to transformative learning. The first is limited peer exposure. Students are often confined to interacting with their peers only within their institution or major, stifling creativity, diversity of thought, and global perspective. The second is weak interdisciplinary training. Despite strong evidence that exposure to multiple disciplinary insights strengthens students’ problem-solving skills, most academic programs and institutions are siloed. The findings from several scientific studies suggest that peer-to-peer instruction and exposure to ideas from diverse disciplines improve students’ overall learning and problem-solving abilities.

For instance, researchers at the University of Arizona’s Department of Educational Psychology (Tullis & Goldstone, 2020) found that: students retain more and perform better when they actively discuss concepts with diverse peers. Similarly, researchers at Harvard (Crouch et al. 2007), Columbia (Stockwell et al. 2015), and Cornell (Bennoun & Holm, 2021) reached similar conclusions.


Yet, how can dynamic exchanges across geography, culture, and disciplines take place among students who don’t know each other or share a prior network? We created KampuLynk to answer this very question.

KampuLynk is an online educational platform designed to connect university students – across majors, institutions, and countries – to exchange ideas, collaborate on projects, and access educational resources to facilitate their growth. It’s a platform to connect with like-minded (or cross-disciplinary) students, a place to store and share academic notes, and a space to collaborate, chat, learn, and grow.
With Kampulynk, a computer science student at Columbia University can connect and exchange ideas with students with the same or different major(s) at say, the University of London, Peking University, or the University of Cape Town (South Africa), even without a prior connection.
